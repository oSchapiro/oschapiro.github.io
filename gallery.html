Wonders of the World

The Wonders of the World are multiple lists of wonders, natural and man-made that have been compiled to document the most amazing wonders. The three lists shown in this page are the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, the Seven Wonders of Nature and the Seven Wonders of the Industrial World.

Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids of Giza. It is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, and the only one to remain largely intact.

Mount Everest

Mount Everest is the tallest mountain on earth. It is part of the Himalayan mountain range and is 29029ft tall from peak to sea level. It was first measured to be 29,000ft but it was thought that such an exact number would not be believed by the public, so it was decided to report its height as 29,002ft.

Hoover Dam

Hoover Dam is located in the Black Canyon of the Colorado River on the border between Arizona and Nevada. Construction began in 1931 and ended in 1936. The cost of construction totalled $49 million or $833 million after adjusting for inflation.
